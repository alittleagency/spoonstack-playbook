what spoonstack is

An AI second brain for neurodivergent adults — AuDHD, ADHD, autistic, late-diagnosed women especially. Built by Brittany May for her own brain because nothing else worked.

the core promise

"your brain works differently. your second brain should too." spoonstack learns your patterns, handles your executive function overhead, and surfaces the right thing at the right moment — without making you organize anything.

what it's NOT

Not a productivity app. Not a task manager. Not a habit tracker. Not Notion. Not Obsidian. Not Apple Notes with AI bolted on.

what it IS

A second brain that captures anything, organizes nothing, and brings the right thing back at the right moment. Care, not tracking.

business

$79/year early access. price locked forever for early users. manual approval gate — Brittany flips is_approved in Supabase. 20 beta users from Threads ready to come in. launch: june 15 2026.

the brain-first epiphany

brittany had this insight on june 15 based on the ND brain architecture research. it changes the product direction. discuss before building anything new.

open question · june 15 2026
"what if the brain is the front door and widgets are background agents?"

right now: users navigate to stacks → open widget tiles → get help. the stacks are the UI.

new direction: the brain is the UI. you dump everything there. background agents (which are what widgets are) detect what you need and run automatically or on demand from within the brain. stacks still exist but they're infrastructure, not navigation.

the home screen shows a few pinned shortcuts for things you reach for constantly (dinner decider, order of operations, work with me). everything else lives inside the brain.

this is the most important product decision to make before building anything else. the ND research backs it: ND brains go to search, not folders. the brain IS the product.

✦questions to answer: what does the home screen show? how do agents get triggered? what about widgets you open intentionally (regulator)? are stacks gone or just backgrounded?

ND brain architecture research — key findings

synthesized from r/ADHD, r/AuDHD, r/ObsidianMD and academic sources. these findings shape every product decision.

the surprising finding

the real competitor isn't Obsidian or Notion. it's Apple Notes — because it's already open. spoonstack wins if it captures as fast as Apple Notes and surfaces things better. that's it.

3 modes of touching the brain
active retrieval — "I need something right now." ND brains search by feeling and context, not category.

passive surfacing — "show me something I forgot." situational, not scheduled. never mid-task.

serendipitous discovery — "I forgot I saved this." once a week memory card. pure delight.
3 kill conditions
capture requires a decision — any required field loses the thought.

AI sounds like it's judging — "I've noticed you tend to..." = immediate withdrawal.

silence framed as failure — no streaks, no "you haven't checked in," no accumulating unanswered questions.
✦AuDHD users score highest on monotropism — deepest attention tunnels, hardest transitions. never interrupt a user mid-task to surface something. all surfacing happens at natural transition points only.
