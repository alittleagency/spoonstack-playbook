EPIPHANY · JUNE 15

brain-first direction

the core insight

The brain should be the front door, not the stacks. Widgets work better as background agents triggered from brain dumps than as things you navigate to directly. The home screen stops being a stack grid and becomes the capture interface. The brain tab is where your entries live. Stacks still exist but are backgrounded.

the mental model

capture (home screen, always primary) → inbox (AI processes and auto-sorts, temporary waiting room) → brain (permanent, searchable, organized by life domain)

The inbox is not a permanent home. It's a queue. Things land there, get AI processing, and graduate into the brain. The AI restates items in the brain's voice — not "where did my scissors go" but "did you find your good scissors? last time it was ruby who had them."

what changed
Home screen default route now leads with capture bar. Bottom nav: home · people · brain · stacks. 4 tabs, locked, no exceptions. Stacks still accessible but not the primary entry point.
what stayed
Stacks exist as infrastructure. Widgets still work. Bottom sheet still opens per widget. Routes unchanged. Stacks are the back room, not the front door.
ambiguity + multi-widget dumps
When a brain dump contains 2+ items or unclear intent, /api/ai/inbox-process extracts each item, restates it in the brain's voice, assigns urgency and category, and surfaces them as inbox task rows.
never interrupt a tunnel
AuDHD brains have deep attention tunnels. Brain surfacing only happens at natural transition points — opening a stack, finishing a widget, explicitly visiting the brain tab. Never mid-task.

BUILT · REPORTS ARE STUBS

brain tab

what's built vs. what's stub

Brain tab uses BottomNav (4 tabs) — not Phase2Nav or any 6-tab variant. River model is live. Left nav, filter pills, and report stub pages are deployed. Weekly summary report: stub only. Connection map: stub only.

what's deployed

river model — entries flow as a vertical river, recency-first. Clusters emerge from colored headers and spine lines.

left nav — domain categories listed vertically. Tap to filter to that domain.

filter pills — horizontal scroll above river. Filter by tag, category, or date range.

report stub pages — weekly summary and connection map exist as routes but are placeholder UI only.

search behavior
Currently lexical. Semantic search is planned — "the thing about Jackson's food" needs to find it. This is the key differentiator from Apple Notes and a P1 backlog item.
default sort
Recency first, always. "This week" then "earlier." Timestamps always visible. ND brains remember by time and context, not category.
crash mode
Low spoon: banner "keeping things simple." Search bar only + 3 most recent entries. No clusters, no question, no connections. "the brain asks nothing on hard days."
4-tab nav rule
Brain tab uses the same BottomNav as every other tab. 4 tabs only: home · people · brain · stacks. The Phase2Nav (6-tab) component is removed and must not be re-introduced.

interaction model
Tap any node → entries slide up from bottom in detail panel. Tap same node again → close. Tap elsewhere → close. One cluster at a time. Never the whole graph. Center node always visible: "your brain" with ✦.
why not a full graph
The hairball problem — hundreds of entries shown at once = unusable. ND brains need one tunnel at a time. One cluster at a time respects monotropism. The galaxy feels like Obsidian without the navigation anxiety.
search still works
Search bar at top always available. Typing highlights the matching node. You never have to navigate — you can always just search.
mobile access
Galaxy opens as full-screen overlay via ✦ button on the brain tab. Stream is still the mobile default. Galaxy is optional, beautiful, explorable.
HOME SCREEN FEATURES

"while you have a minute" + nudge

"while you have a minute" — filtering rules

Shows brain entries from the last 90 days only. Filtered to actionable entries — must contain action language ("need to", "follow up", "call", "schedule", "check", "remind") OR be tagged with #todo, #followUp, #action.

If no entries match the filter, the section hides entirely — never shows an empty state, never shows a placeholder. The section simply doesn't render.

"spoonstack is curious" nudge

Shows on home screen below "while you have a minute." One question at a time, derived from the user's own brain entries, framed as curiosity — never as a task or a prompt.

Responses save to brain_entries with tag ["nudge-response"]. The question hash is stored in localStorage to prevent the same question appearing twice. User can swipe away with no consequence — no tracking, no judgment.

PDA-safe framing rule

The nudge question must never feel like a demand. "the brain's been wondering..." not "you should think about..." or "have you considered...". One question only. Never more than one visible at once. Never scheduled. Never tracked beyond the hash.

FROM RESEARCH

ND design principles

the most important finding

The real competitor is Apple Notes — not Obsidian, not Notion. ND brains return to Apple Notes because it has zero decision cost at capture. spoonstack wins if it captures as fast as Apple Notes and surfaces things better than it. Simplicity first. Intelligence second.

01
capture must be frictionless
One tap from anywhere. No required fields. No "where does this go?" moment. Under 3 seconds total. One hesitation = thought lost = trust broken. Users won't give a second chance.
02
organization is a gift, never a requirement
AI suggests routing. User confirms or ignores. Nothing forces categorization. Items stay in inbox indefinitely without penalty. The system organizes around the user's capacity, not a schedule.
03
design for the bad day first
Low spoon mode is not an edge case — it's a regular Tuesday. Brain tab at CRISIS shows 2-3 entries max. All language softens. No suggestions, no questions, just retrieval.
04
recency is the primary retrieval cue
ND brains remember by context and time, not category. Default sort is always recency. Always show timestamps. "What did I save this week?" beats "what's in my health folder?"
05
the brain delivers — the user doesn't navigate
Relevant brain entries surface in stack context automatically. Brain surfacing only at natural transition points — never mid-task (monotropism). The tunnel is sacred.
06
care, not surveillance
Use specific language — "this reminds me of..." not "I've noticed you tend to..." One instance of feeling surveilled and users stop sharing real things. The brain becomes a performance, not a brain.
07
one thing at a time, always
One question. One surfaced memory. One connection. One entry per context. Restraint is care. Galaxy shows one cluster at a time, not the whole graph.
08
never make silence feel like failure
No streaks. No completion percentages. No "you haven't checked in in 5 days." The brain feels equally welcoming whether the user was here yesterday or three weeks ago.
09
hold the past gently
Old entries are treasure. Interest cycling is real — hyperfixations hibernate and return. Surface past entries as memories, not archives. Zero judgment always.
10
semantic search, not keyword search
"The thing about the hard week" should find it. "The idea I had when I was anxious" should find it. Zero failed retrieval moments is the goal.
do this
  • capture bar always primary, always reachable
  • one question in the brain at a time
  • swipe to dismiss anything — no confirmation
  • "the brain's been wondering..." not "answer this"
  • show timestamps always
  • recency sort as default
  • crash mode dramatically simpler
  • voice as equal-status capture option
never do this
  • streaks, completion %, check-in tracking
  • "I've noticed you tend to..."
  • "you haven't checked in in N days"
  • required fields at capture
  • interrupt mid-task to surface a memory
  • scheduled mandatory check-ins
  • more than one question visible at once
  • show the full graph at once

UNRESOLVED

open questions

product

Does voice capture actually replace text capture for ND moms, or does playback anxiety reduce usage? What's the right threshold for AI-suggested connections — how many entries before connections feel useful vs. overwhelming? At what cadence does a "from a while back" memory card delight vs. feel like the app is time-traveling on you?

session state architecture

How should mood and context propagate across widgets? Shared React context? Supabase session row? If user sets mood at check-in, it should persist for the whole session without re-asking. What's the right TTL on mood context before it expires?

beta

How many users before we can validate the brain-first direction? What do we watch for — are they using the stream, the stacks, or just the capture bar? If users stop going to stacks after 2 weeks, brain-first is confirmed. If they still go to stacks, the direction needs revision.
