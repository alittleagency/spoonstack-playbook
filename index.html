EPIPHANY · JUNE 15

brain-first direction

the core insight

The brain should be the front door, not the stacks. Widgets work better as background agents triggered from brain dumps than as things you navigate to directly. The home screen stops being a stack grid and becomes the capture interface. The brain tab is where your entries live. Stacks still exist but are backgrounded.

the mental model

capture (home screen, always primary) → inbox (AI processes and auto-sorts, temporary waiting room) → brain (permanent, searchable, organized by life domain)

The inbox is not a permanent home. It's a queue. Things land there, get AI processing, and graduate into the brain. The AI restates items in the brain's voice — not "where did my scissors go" but "did you find your good scissors? last time it was ruby who had them."

what changed
Home screen default route now leads with capture bar. Bottom nav: home · brain · people · stacks. Stacks still accessible but not the primary entry point.
what stayed
Stacks exist as infrastructure. 29 widgets still work. Bottom sheet still opens per widget. Routes unchanged. Stacks are the back room, not the front door.
ambiguity + multi-widget dumps
When a brain dump contains 2+ items or unclear intent, /api/ai/inbox-process extracts each item, restates it in the brain's voice, assigns urgency and category, and surfaces them as inbox task rows.
never interrupt a tunnel
AuDHD brains have deep attention tunnels. Brain surfacing only happens at natural transition points — opening a stack, finishing a widget, explicitly visiting the brain tab. Never mid-task.

Raw dump → brain restatement. "need to call robin" → "call robin back about the job." "cant find my scissors" → "did you find your good scissors? last time it was ruby who had them." DESIGNED · NOT YET BUILT

brain tab spec

the core insight from research

The real competitor is Apple Notes, not Obsidian. spoonstack wins if it captures as fast as Apple Notes and surfaces things better than it. The brain tab doesn't need to be powerful — it needs to feel effortless. Search is the hero. Everything else is secondary.

three views of the same brain

stream (mobile default) — dark header anchors search, light body for reading. Entries flow recency-first. Clusters emerge from colored cluster headers + spine lines. Question queue as gold ambient card. Domain filter chips scroll horizontally.

wiki (desktop default) — sidebar with people and topics. Main panel shows wiki page for selected person/topic. Facts the brain has extracted from entries + raw entries below. Organized but self-built — no templates required.

galaxy (visual overlay) — 12 vision-board category nodes radiating from center "your brain." Tap a node to see its entries bloom in a detail panel. One cluster at a time. Never the whole graph at once. See galaxy section below.

search behavior
Semantic, not keyword. "The thing about Jackson's food" must find it. "The idea I had when I was anxious" must find it. Placeholder: "search by feeling, fragment, anything." Always focused on open.
default sort
Recency first, always. "This week" then "earlier." Never by category or alphabetically. Timestamps always visible. "You saved this 3 days ago" beats any folder tree.
question queue
"✦ the brain's been wondering" — one question, derived from user's own entries, framed as curiosity. "swipe away · no pressure · no tracking." Never scheduled, never tracked, never more than one. PDA-safe framing always.
crash mode
Low spoon: banner "keeping things simple." Search bar only + 3 most recent entries. No clusters, no question, no connections. "the brain asks nothing on hard days." Never triggered automatically — detected from spoon level or explicit low-spoon toggle.

galaxy view spec

the 12 nodes

Vision-board categories, not app categories. These are how Brittany actually thinks about her life at a high level. Entries get AI-sorted into these buckets on the way through the inbox.

interaction model
Tap any node → entries slide up from bottom in detail panel. Tap same node again → close. Tap elsewhere → close. One cluster at a time. Never the whole graph. Center node always visible: "your brain" with ✦.
why not a full graph
The hairball problem — 352 entries shown at once = unusable. ND brains need one tunnel at a time. One cluster at a time respects monotropism. The galaxy feels like Obsidian without the navigation anxiety.
search still works
Search bar at top always available. Typing highlights the matching node. You never have to navigate — you can always just search.
mobile access
Galaxy opens as full-screen overlay via ✦ button on the brain tab. Stream is still the mobile default. Galaxy is optional, beautiful, explorable.
LOCKED

decisions made this session

01
brain tab is NOT the home screen

Home screen = capture + pinned + inbox. Brain tab = where your entries live. Separate nav destinations. Never confuse them in code or copy.

02
desktop brain is wiki-only, no separate stream view

Desktop defaults to wiki. Stream is mobile. Toggle between wiki and galaxy on desktop. No "desktop stream" as a third view.

03
inbox items must sound like the brain knows you

Not the raw dump. The brain's restatement. Always uses what's in memory (jackson's safe foods, who had the scissors, how long since you talked to robin). This is what separates spoonstack from Apple Notes.

04
no colored pill backgrounds in inbox

Dots only for color. Time label gets the accent color. Row background stays neutral. The color signals category, not urgency level.

05
light theme throughout — no dark brain view

Warm dark (#1A1714) is reserved for the capture bar and the wiki/stream dark header. The brain content area is always light. Not negotiable.

06
galaxy nodes = vision board categories

12 nodes: ideas · questions · home · family · friends · work · fun · rest · active projects · wealth · health · faith. These are life domains, not app domains. Faith is peach (#C4713A).

07
beta sequence locked

FROM RESEARCH

ND design principles

the most important finding

The real competitor is Apple Notes — not Obsidian, not Notion. ND brains return to Apple Notes because it has zero decision cost at capture. spoonstack wins if it captures as fast as Apple Notes and surfaces things better than it. Simplicity first. Intelligence second.

01
capture must be frictionless
One tap from anywhere. No required fields. No "where does this go?" moment. Under 3 seconds total. One hesitation = thought lost = trust broken. Users won't give a second chance.
02
organization is a gift, never a requirement
AI suggests routing. User confirms or ignores. Nothing forces categorization. Items stay in inbox indefinitely without penalty. The system organizes around the user's capacity, not a schedule.
03
design for the bad day first
Low spoon mode is not an edge case — it's a regular Tuesday. Brain tab at CRISIS shows 2-3 entries max. All language softens. No suggestions, no questions, just retrieval.
04
recency is the primary retrieval cue
ND brains remember by context and time, not category. Default sort is always recency. Always show timestamps. "What did I save this week?" beats "what's in my health folder?"
05
the brain delivers — the user doesn't navigate
Relevant brain entries surface in stack context automatically. Brain surfacing only at natural transition points — never mid-task (monotropism). The tunnel is sacred.
06
care, not surveillance
Use specific language — "this reminds me of..." not "I've noticed you tend to..." One instance of feeling surveilled and users stop sharing real things. The brain becomes a performance, not a brain.
07
one thing at a time, always
One question. One surfaced memory. One connection. One entry per context. Restraint is care. Galaxy shows one cluster at a time, not the whole graph.
08
never make silence feel like failure
No streaks. No completion percentages. No "you haven't checked in in 5 days." The brain feels equally welcoming whether the user was here yesterday or three weeks ago.
09
hold the past gently
Old entries are treasure. Interest cycling is real — hyperfixations hibernate and return. Surface past entries as memories, not archives. Zero judgment always.
10
semantic search, not keyword search
"The thing about the hard week" should find it. "The idea I had when I was anxious" should find it. Zero failed retrieval moments is the goal.
do this
  • capture bar always primary, always reachable
  • one question in the brain at a time
  • swipe to dismiss anything — no confirmation
  • "the brain's been wondering..." not "answer this"
  • show timestamps always
  • recency sort as default
  • crash mode dramatically simpler
  • voice as equal-status capture option
never do this
  • streaks, completion %, check-in tracking
  • "I've noticed you tend to..."
  • "you haven't checked in in N days"
  • required fields at capture
  • interrupt mid-task to surface a memory
  • scheduled mandatory check-ins
  • more than one question visible at once
  • show the full graph at once

UNRESOLVED

open questions

product

Does voice capture actually replace text capture for ND moms, or does playback anxiety reduce usage? What's the right threshold for AI-suggested connections — how many entries before connections feel useful vs. overwhelming? Does "this entry is connected to 3 others" as a badge create pressure to review those connections? At what cadence does a "from a while back" memory card delight vs. feel like the app is time-traveling on you?

architecture

"ask brain" button on home — does it open a dedicated chat-style view, or expand the capture bar inline into a back-and-forth? Pinned tiles grid — 2×2 for 4, or scrollable horizontal for unlimited? Lapsed-contact query — when should we add a real last_contact field to brain_entries vs. deriving from widget_responses?

beta

How many users before we can validate the brain-first direction? What do we watch for — are they using the stream, the stacks, or just the capture bar? If users stop going to stacks after 2 weeks, brain-first is confirmed. If they still go to stacks, the direction needs revision.
