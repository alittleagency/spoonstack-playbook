customer journey

three complete user paths — what they see, what happens in the system, what's broken, and what's missing. written against the live codebase.

J1
new paid user
finds spoonstack.app → pays $79 → waits for approval → gets welcome email → onboards → uses the app for the first time
Step 01
lands on spoonstack.app exists

She found it somehow — probably a link from someone who told her about it. The root / route rewrites to public/landing.html, a single-file landing page served as static HTML.

She sees: a bold headline about the app, a brief description about it being built for neurodivergent moms, and a "get early access" CTA button. There's a demo section showing some widget previews. The page uses the same gold + cream design language as the app.

"an AI second brain for neurodivergent moms who are holding everything together."
could go wrong

She might not understand what a "stack" or "widget" is. The concept is novel and the language is product-specific. She may not know what she's paying for.

missing right now

No real testimonials. No "here's what happens after you sign up" flow. No FAQ. No visible price before the CTA. No mobile-optimized screenshots of the actual app.

would make it better

Show the capture bar and inbox in action. One paragraph that explains the approval process upfront so she doesn't feel blindsided by /pending. Show the $79 price on the landing page.

Step 02
clicks "get early access" → stripe checkout exists

The CTA links directly to a Stripe checkout URL (pre-configured). She enters name, email, and payment details. Stripe handles everything — she never creates an account before paying.

Stripe checkout is hosted on stripe.com. She's leaving the spoonstack domain entirely. The product name and price ($79) appear in the Stripe UI. After payment Stripe redirects her to /api/stripe/success?session_id=….

could go wrong

She abandons at the price. She doesn't recognize "alittle.agency" on the Stripe merchant name. She's paying before ever creating an account, which feels backward — no account = no confirmation email from the app.

missing right now

No Stripe-branded order confirmation email from spoonstack (Stripe sends its own generic receipt). No "what happens next" copy at checkout. No account creation before payment.

would make it better

Create the account first (sign up → pay → onboard). Or at minimum set a custom Stripe receipt email with spoonstack branding and a clear "you'll hear from brittany within 24 hours" message.

Step 03
payment confirmed — stripe webhook fires exists

Two things happen simultaneously after successful payment:

1. The browser redirect. Stripe sends the browser to /api/stripe/success with ?session_id=. The success route fetches the Stripe session, reads the client_reference_id (user ID) and customer.email, updates profiles.is_paid = true in Supabase, and sets a ss-paid=1 cookie so middleware immediately passes /onboarding and /display. Then it redirects to /onboarding.

2. The Stripe webhook. /api/stripe/webhook receives checkout.session.completed asynchronously, also sets is_paid = true (idempotent safety net), and sends the welcome email via Resend.

Problem: She hasn't created a Supabase account yet. The client_reference_id would be null because there's no user session. The success route can't update profiles.is_paid without a user ID. The webhook tries customer.email as a fallback, but if the profile doesn't exist yet, there's nothing to update.

could go wrong

If she pays before creating an account, client_reference_id is null. The webhook can't mark her as paid. She signs up later and starts the free flow instead of the paid flow. This is the biggest gap in the system.

missing right now

The flow assumes she already has a Supabase account when she pays. There's no reconciliation if she doesn't. No way to match a Stripe payment to a future signup by email unless the webhook stores the paid email in a separate lookup table.

would make it better

Pre-registration flow: email entry before Stripe → create a placeholder profile → pass user ID as client_reference_id → then pay. Or: store paid emails in a stripe_purchases table and check it on account creation.

Step 04
lands on /pending exists

After paying, she lands here. The page is sparse and honest:

✦

you're in. almost.

your account is being set up. brittany will send you a welcome email within 24 hours and you'll have full access.

sit tight — it's worth it.

There's a gold accent dot, a gold tint callout, and a monospace note to check her spam folder. The page doesn't require authentication — it's purely informational.

could go wrong

She doesn't know if the payment actually worked. There's no "payment confirmed for $79" confirmation on this page. She may think something went wrong and try to pay again.

missing right now

No payment receipt summary. No estimated wait time. No way to check her status. No "add this email to your contacts" prompt. No social proof that others are waiting too. No option to submit her email if she arrived here without paying.

would make it better

"Payment of $79 received. ✓" confirmation at the top. "Brittany approves accounts manually — usually same day on weekdays." A small "what to expect" timeline so she knows the approval isn't instant.

Step 05
approval — automatic for paid users ✓ shipped june 18

Fixed june 18 2026. The Stripe webhook (/api/stripe/webhook) now sets both is_paid = true and is_approved = true in a single update. Paid users are approved the moment the webhook fires — no manual intervention by Brittany, no waiting period.

Before this fix, the webhook only set is_paid = true. Brittany had to manually run SQL in Supabase to set is_approved = true. She was the bottleneck for every paid signup.

Beta users (no Stripe) are still manually approved via POST /api/admin/approve — see Journey 2.

could still go wrong

If client_reference_id is null (user paid before creating a Supabase account), the webhook can't find the profile to update. This is the remaining critical gap — see step 3.

still missing

No way for the user to see that she's been approved in real time. /pending doesn't poll. She has to refresh manually or wait for the welcome email to arrive and click the link.

would make it better

/pending polling every 30 seconds that auto-redirects to /onboarding when both flags are true. Would close the loop without requiring any action from the user.

Step 06
approval email ✓ shipped june 18

Updated june 18 2026. The Stripe webhook sends an approval email via Resend after setting is_paid = true and is_approved = true. Because approval is now instant for paid users, the email fires within seconds of payment — not hours later. The old email copy was too long and marketing-heavy. New copy is short and functional:

from: Brittany at SpoonStack <brittany@alittle.agency>
subject: you're in. welcome to spoonstack.
your account is approved and ready. sign in at spoonstack.app/sign-in and your brain is waiting. — brittany

The same sendApprovalEmail function in lib/email.ts is also called by POST /api/admin/approve when Brittany manually approves a beta user — same email, same copy, single source of truth.

remaining risk

The email fires on checkout.session.completed only. If she doesn't have a Supabase account yet (paid before signing up), the webhook can't find her profile — no profile update, no email. She's in limbo.

still missing

The email links to /sign-in but she may not have a password yet (if she paid before signing up). No explicit "you'll need to create an account first" guidance. No HTML email template — plain text only.

would make it better

Add one line: "if you haven't created your account yet, start at spoonstack.app/sign-up." Removes the confusion for the pay-first, signup-second edge case.

Step 07
first login — /sign-in exists

She clicks the sign-in link from the welcome email. /sign-in is a Supabase Auth email+password form. If this is her first time, she has to sign up first — the sign-in page links to /sign-up.

After authenticating, the middleware reads her profile. If onboarding_complete is false, she's redirected to /onboarding. If complete but not approved, she lands on /pending. If both approved and onboarding-complete, she goes to /display.

could go wrong

She clicks sign-in but hasn't created an account yet. The sign-in form fails with "invalid credentials." She doesn't notice the sign-up link. She may think her account should have been created automatically when she paid.

missing right now

No magic link login. No "first time? create your account here" explanation in the welcome email. No SSO. No password reset link prominently placed. The sign-up and sign-in are separate pages — a new user may not realize they need to create a separate account after paying.

would make it better

Magic link login: "enter your email and we'll send you a link." No password needed on first use. The welcome email should say explicitly: "you'll need to create a password at sign-up — this is a separate step from payment."

Step 08
onboarding — 3 input screens exists

Three sequential screens, each seeding the brain. Progress dots at the top (1 of 3, 2 of 3, 3 of 3). Every screen has a skip option. All entries save as brain_entries rows.

Screen 1 — who's in your world?
"spoonstack works better when it knows your people." Partner name + "one thing to know: ___". Up to 4 kids, each with name + note. Up to N close friends with name + note. Button: "looks good →" / skip: "I'll add this later →"
Saves to brain_entries with category family or people.

Screen 2 — what's coming up?
"anything on your plate this week I should know about." Large textarea. Placeholder: "monday: dentist 2pm. simone has cheer tuesday at 4. permission slip due friday. need to call insurance." Button: "got it →" / skip: "nothing yet →"
Saves as category schedule.

Screen 3 — what's been on your mind?
"tasks, things to remember, stuff you've been meaning to do." Large textarea. Placeholder: "call the insurance company, noah's meds need a refill, haven't texted robin back in 3 weeks, need to figure out camp for the kids" Button: "saving it →" / skip: "nothing yet →"
Saves as category general. On save, sets profiles.onboarding_complete = true.

could go wrong

She skips all three screens. Her brain is empty. /display shows nothing relevant. She has no idea what the app does. She churns in 48 hours because there was nothing there.

missing right now

No onboarding for what the app actually does. Screens 1-3 seed data but never explain how it's used. She doesn't know what a "brain entry" is, what widgets do, or where to go first. The onboarding is pure input, no output preview.

would make it better

After screen 2, show a single example: "here's what spoonstack did with what you just told it." A sample widget response using her real data. Make the value tangible before she even reaches /display.

Step 09
"your brain is ready." — screen 4 exists

After screen 3 saves, the app transitions to a full-bleed dark screen (background #1A1714) — the only dark moment in the entire app. A gold ✦ symbol, then:

your brain is ready.

spoonstack knows your family now.
it'll get smarter every time you use it.

A gold "go →" button advances to /display. This screen is the emotional payoff — the product promise made real. onboarding_complete was already set to true before this screen renders. The "go →" button calls refreshProfile() then routes to /display.

could go wrong

She skipped all three screens. The message "spoonstack knows your family now" feels false — her brain is empty and the app knows nothing. The emotional payoff lands hollow.

missing right now

No personalization here. It could say her first name, reference something she added ("we've got Bella, Jackson, and Ruby in there"), or show a preview of what she told it. It's the same copy for everyone.

would make it better

"your brain is ready, [first name]." + "we've got [kid names] and [this week's schedule] in there. spoonstack will surface what matters." Personalized with actual data she just entered.

Step 10
first landing on /display exists

She lands on the home screen. She sees, top to bottom:

HomeHeader — current time (large), today's date, weather (Atlanta, fetched from wttr.in), and a moon phase SVG with next new/full moon dates.

GreetingCard — "good morning." greeting, today's family schedule events (pulled from brain_entries with time patterns), tomorrow preview.

CaptureBar — "what's on your mind?" input. The central interaction point.

SmartInbox — recent unprocessed brain entries waiting for attention.

PinnedWidgets — 4 quick-access tiles (partner briefing, regulator, ask the vault, out of sight). Customizable.

CuriousCard — spoonstack surfaces a question or connection from her brain entries.

Phase2Nav — bottom nav: home, out of sight, people, brain, a minute, shelf.

could go wrong

Weather fails silently (she's not in Atlanta). SmartInbox is empty on day 1 because entries from onboarding haven't been "processed" yet. PinnedWidgets shows generic defaults she doesn't recognize. She has no idea what to do first.

missing right now

No "start here" nudge for new users. No first-time tooltip or highlighted CTA. No indication that the capture bar is the primary action. The GreetingCard may show no family events if onboarding was skipped. The CuriousCard may be empty or generic on day 1.

would make it better

First-visit overlay or highlighted capture bar with copy: "start here — type anything." Show an example entry being processed. On day 1, the SmartInbox should show her onboarding entries as a preview of what's possible.

Step 11
first capture — capture bar exists

She types something in the capture bar — "need to call the pediatrician about noah's refill" — and hits enter or the send button.

The app calls /api/brain/extract or /api/brain/route to classify the entry. An AI prompt categorizes it (likely logistics or hard-thing), extracts a clean title, and saves it to brain_entries. A "saved ✦" toast appears.

The SmartInbox updates to show the new entry. The entry may appear in the "what needs doing" widget on the "a minute" stack if categorized correctly.

could go wrong

The AI classification fails or times out. The entry saves with category "general" instead of something actionable. She doesn't know the entry was saved or what happened to it. No feedback beyond the toast.

missing right now

No visible "filed under [category]" confirmation after saving. No way to see where the entry went immediately after capture. No undo. The save is silent beyond the toast — she doesn't know if it'll resurface or disappear.

would make it better

After capture: brief "filed as logistics — will resurface in what needs doing" chip. Makes the system legible. She learns the model by using it.

Step 12
first widget use exists

She taps one of the pinned widgets — most likely the "regulator" (red, familiar RSD/overwhelm framing) or "partner briefing" (gold, practical utility). She lands on the stack page for that widget.

On the stack page she sees the widget tile grid. She taps the widget, a bottom sheet slides up. She types something, taps the submit button, and waits 1-3 seconds for the AI response.

The response appears with a left accent border. She sees two action pills: "that helps" and "save to brain." She either acts on the response or closes the sheet.

could go wrong

The AI response is generic or misses her context entirely (her brain entries weren't used as context). The 15-second timeout fires and she sees an error. She doesn't know what "save to brain" does. The response doesn't feel personalized enough to justify $79.

missing right now

No indicator that her brain context is being used. No way to see what context was sent. No history of past widget interactions. No "try this next" prompt. Widgets are one-shot — there's no conversation thread.

would make it better

Small "✦ using your brain" badge when brain context is present. After a good response: "save to brain" should feel rewarding — maybe animate a small ✦ flying toward the brain nav icon. Show that the memory grew.

Step 13
comes back the next day partial

She opens the app the next morning. She's already logged in (Supabase session persists). She lands directly on /display.

What's different: the time and greeting update. Her SmartInbox shows yesterday's entries that haven't been resolved. The GreetingCard may show today's schedule events if she captured anything with times. The CuriousCard may surface something from her brain.

What she probably does: checks the inbox, captures something new, maybe taps a widget.

could go wrong

Everything looks the same as day 1. The inbox feels like clutter she didn't process. CuriousCard shows a generic question. She doesn't feel like the app "remembers" her from yesterday. The momentum stalls.

missing right now

No "good morning, here's what you had going yesterday" summary. No "you saved 3 things yesterday" recap. No proactive surfacing of yesterday's unresolved entries. The app looks identical on day 2 as day 1 unless she actively uses the brain tab.

would make it better

Day 2 greeting variant: "you left 2 things unresolved yesterday — the pediatrician call and the permission slip. want to handle one now?" Spoonstack should feel like it held her place overnight.

J2
free beta user
gets a dm from brittany → signs up free → manually approved → same onboarding experience as paid user
Step 01
DM from brittany — the invite exists

Brittany messages her directly (Instagram, iMessage, or wherever they're connected). She says something like: "hey, I built something for brains like ours — want to try it? it's free for you." Includes a direct link to /sign-up.

This is a warm, personal invite. The user trusts the source implicitly. There's no landing page friction — she goes straight to sign-up because she already wants to try it.

could go wrong

She doesn't know what she signed up for beyond "something for our kind of brain." There's no product context before sign-up. She may set expectations too high or too different from reality.

missing right now

No beta-specific landing page or invite token system. If Brittany shares the link publicly by mistake, anyone could sign up for free. No way to distinguish "Brittany's person" from a random stranger.

would make it better

Unique invite link per person with a token (/sign-up?invite=xyz). Hitting the link stores the token, which gets associated with the signup. Makes tracking easier and prevents unintended free signups.

Step 02
signs up — no stripe exists

She hits /sign-up, enters email + password. Supabase creates the user account. The profile row is inserted. A trigger fires /api/notify-signup (or this is called client-side on sign-up success) which emails Brittany with the new user's info and the SQL to approve her.

is_paid = false and is_approved = false by default. Middleware sends her to /pending after signup until Brittany flips both flags.

could go wrong

The notify-signup API call fails silently. Brittany never gets the notification. The user sits on /pending forever without knowing Brittany didn't get the email.

missing right now

No confirmation that the signup notification was received. No ETA on the /pending page specific to beta users. No way for Brittany to see all pending users without going to Supabase.

would make it better

Brittany should have a simple admin page at /admin showing pending users with one-click approve. Better than running SQL in the Supabase console every time.

Step 03
approval via API — no more supabase SQL ✓ shipped june 18

Updated june 18 2026. Brittany no longer needs to open the Supabase dashboard. She can approve a beta user with a single curl or fetch call from her terminal or a simple script:

POST /api/admin/approve
Authorization: Bearer <brittany-access-token>
{ "userId": "<user-id-from-notify-email>" }

The endpoint verifies the caller is Brittany's user ID (4f29c0a6-…), sets is_approved = true on the profile, looks up the user's email from Supabase auth, and sends the same approval email that paid users receive. Returns 403 for anyone else.

Beta users still need both is_paid and is_approved set to true. The notify-signup email Brittany receives already includes the userId — she copies it into the fetch call. The user ID comes from /api/notify-signup which fires on sign-up.

could go wrong

Brittany's access token expires between seeing the email and running the approval. She'd need to sign in again to get a fresh token. Still manual — just less friction than SQL.

still missing

No /pending polling for the user — she still doesn't know she's been approved until she refreshes. No admin UI. Still needs is_paid to also be set for beta users (separate SQL or a second param on the endpoint).

would make it better

Add is_paid as an optional param on the approve endpoint so beta approval is truly one call. And /pending polling so the user auto-redirects the moment approval fires.

Step 04
onboarding — identical to paid user exists

Once both flags are true, the next time she signs in (or refreshes), the middleware routes her to /onboarding. She goes through the exact same 4-screen onboarding flow as a paid user.

Screen 1: her people. Screen 2: what's coming up. Screen 3: what's on her mind. Screen 4: "your brain is ready." → /display.

There's no distinction in the onboarding between a beta user and a paid user. Same copy, same screens, same brain seeding.

could go wrong

She skips everything because Brittany told her what to do via DM and she thought "I'll just use the app." She arrives at /display with an empty brain and no guidance.

missing right now

No "Brittany invited you" personal touch. No beta-specific messaging. The onboarding feels identical to a cold commercial experience when it could feel warm and direct: "hey, it's brittany — let me show you around."

would make it better

If an invite token is passed at signup, show beta-specific onboarding copy: "since you're a founding beta user, here's what we really want to know from you — what's hardest about your week right now?"

Step 05
first use and feedback loop informal

The beta user's first use is identical to a paid user. But there's a crucial difference: Brittany has a relationship with this person. She's a direct feedback channel.

Right now there's no in-app feedback mechanism. No "tell brittany what you think" button. No rating prompt after a widget interaction. No way for the beta user to flag issues without going back to the DM thread.

Feedback happens organically over text or DM. This is intimate and valuable but doesn't scale and creates no structured data about what's working.

could go wrong

Beta user has a great insight at 11pm and doesn't text Brittany because it feels weird. The insight is lost. Or she has a bug and can't reproduce it by the time she remembers to mention it.

missing right now

No in-app feedback. No structured way to capture "this widget helped" vs "this did nothing." No bug report mechanism. No NPS or sentiment pulse.

would make it better

After every AI response: tiny "useful? [yes] [kind of] [no]" — one tap, logged. After a week: "you've used spoonstack 8 times — what's one thing that's helped most? reply in app." Captures insight before it evaporates.

J3
returning user — day 3+
already logged in, already onboarded. opens the app with context from previous days. what does she find, what does she have to hunt for?
Step 01
opens app — lands on /display exists

She opens the app (browser bookmark or home screen shortcut). She's still logged in — Supabase session is persistent. She lands directly on /display, no sign-in screen.

The HomeHeader immediately shows the correct time and weather. The GreetingCard says "good morning" (or afternoon/evening based on hour). If she entered family schedule entries during onboarding or since, they may appear as "today's events" in the greeting card — the app scans brain entries for time patterns.

The spoon budget bar (crisis / low / okay / high) is at the top of the page — she sets her energy level before anything else. This controls which widgets are visible and how the AI responds.

could go wrong

Spoon budget resets to "okay" on every page load (it's not persisted — intentionally resets daily). She forgets to set it and widgets respond as if she has full energy when she doesn't. The GreetingCard family events may not show because entries weren't captured with explicit times.

missing right now

No "how are you doing today?" prompt on open. The spoon budget picker is present but not highlighted — a returning user who forgot about it may skip it. No carry-forward context from yesterday's session (what was she working on? what did she leave unresolved?).

would make it better

On open, if it's been more than 8 hours: "quick — where are you today?" with spoon budget options front and center before anything else loads. Or: detect time of day and prefill a reasonable default ("it's 8am on a Tuesday — probably okay?").

Step 02
smart inbox — entries from yesterday exists

Below the capture bar, the SmartInbox shows recent unprocessed brain entries. These are entries that have been captured but haven't been "acted on" — they've been saved but not filed, not marked done, not addressed by a widget.

The inbox shows entries in reverse chronological order. Each entry has a title (AI-extracted from the raw capture text) and a small category chip. On day 3+ she'll see entries she captured but forgot about: "call the pediatrician" from two days ago, "permission slip due friday" from yesterday.

From the inbox she can: read the entry, tap to expand, or use action pills. The inbox is limited to inboxMax items (3 on low spoons, 5 on okay).

could go wrong

The inbox fills up with everything she's ever captured. On day 7 it shows 5 items but she has 20 unresolved entries. She feels overwhelmed rather than helped. Old entries "done" in her head still show in the inbox because she never marked them resolved in the app.

missing right now

No "swipe to dismiss" or "snooze" on individual inbox items. No visual indication of how old an entry is (age matters — "3 days ago" is very different from "this morning"). No way to bulk-clear the inbox. No surfacing of which entries are time-sensitive vs just lingering.

would make it better

Surface the most time-sensitive first, not just most recent. Age chips: "3 days ago." Swipe left to dismiss, swipe right to send to a widget. "quick resolve" tap that files it as done without opening a widget. The inbox should feel like a triage queue, not a history log.

Step 03
uses the capture bar again exists

She types something new — "ruby's baseball game saturday 10am" or "need to schedule dentist for all three kids" — and submits. The entry is AI-categorized and saved. A toast confirms. It appears in the inbox.

After a few days, the capture bar becomes habitual. She thinks of it as her external brain dump — the place where thoughts go so she doesn't have to hold them. This is the core retention behavior: daily capture = daily return.

could go wrong

She types something complex ("need to prep for IEP meeting thursday, get noah's evaluations together, also email the teacher about the behavior chart") and the AI categorizes it as one entry when it should be three. The nuance is lost.

missing right now

No multi-entry parsing (one dump → multiple categorized entries). No voice input for capture. No photo capture. No reminder to capture things later (e.g., end-of-day capture prompt). The capture bar is great but only works if she remembers to open the app.

would make it better

Multi-entry parsing: "I see 3 things in there — want me to split them up?" Voice capture. A daily evening push notification: "anything on your mind before tomorrow?" Keeps the brain fed without requiring intentional opens.

Step 04
checks the brain tab exists

She taps "brain" in the bottom nav. This is the /brain route. On day 3+ she has a few dozen entries across different domains. The brain shows entries grouped by domain: just-me, my-kids, people, work, projects-hobbies, dont-lose-this.

She can browse by domain (filter tabs at the top), search across entries, look at patterns (the patterns tab), check the journal, and see what's on the map (BrainGraph visualization).

She might tap into "my-kids" to check what's there about each kid, or search for "dentist" to find the entry she captured. The "inbox" tab shows unprocessed captures waiting.

could go wrong

The brain feels overwhelming after 30+ entries with no clear hierarchy. Everything is flat. "just-me" has 18 entries and she can't find the one she wants. The map view (BrainGraph) is beautiful but not actionable — it shows connections but not what to do next.

missing right now

No "ask the brain" free-form search that returns a conversational answer (only exists in the out-of-sight-vault widget, not across all entries). No "stale entries" filter to find things captured weeks ago and never resolved. No summary of "here's what you know about this topic."

would make it better

A global "ask the brain" prompt at the top: "what do you want to remember about ___?" Returns a synthesized answer from all relevant entries. Like asking a very smart assistant who has read all her notes.

Step 05
what spoonstack surfaces vs what she has to find mixed

This is the core UX question: how much does spoonstack work for her vs how much does she have to work the app? On day 3+, here's the honest split:

surfaces automatically

✦ SmartInbox — recent unresolved captures surfaced by default on home screen.

✦ GreetingCard — today's family events parsed from brain entries with time patterns.

✦ CuriousCard — spoonstack picks a question or connection to surface. (Quality depends on brain size and AI context.)

✦ What Needs Doing (/display/while-you-have-a-minute) — pulls actionable entries from logistics/schedule/hard-thing categories and presents them as gentle nudges.

✦ Side Quests — pulls ideas/personal/fun entries and presents them as low-pressure options.

✦ NeedsAttention component — alerts for things that seem to need follow-up based on brain entry patterns.

she has to find

⚠ Most of the brain — domain views, patterns, journal, map. She has to navigate there intentionally.

⚠ Specific entries — search exists but requires knowing what to search for.

⚠ Stack-specific widgets — she has to know which stack to visit. PinnedWidgets helps but only shows 4 tiles. The shelf has everything but is discovery-heavy.

⚠ Old resolved entries — there's no "history" view or "done" queue. Once an entry is resolved, it disappears from view.

⚠ Cross-entry patterns — the patterns tab exists but is AI-generated on demand, not persistent. She may not know to check it.

could go wrong

The surface-to-hunt ratio tips toward hunt. After a week, the brain has 40+ entries. SmartInbox only shows 5. CuriousCard picks one thing. Most of what she captured is sitting in the brain, unsurfaced, unavailable unless she searches.

missing right now

Proactive push ("you mentioned the IEP meeting is Thursday — it's Tuesday. want to prep?"). Time-sensitive surfacing (entries with dates near today should float to the top automatically). A daily digest: "here's what your brain knows about today."

would make it better

The SmartInbox should rank by time-sensitivity, not recency. CuriousCard should be tied to her calendar or schedule entries. The app should earn trust by surfacing things before she has to think to look for them. That's the magic — and it's mostly missing right now.
